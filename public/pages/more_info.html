20/20/20 Timer: Info About Computer Vision Syndrome

Computer Vision Syndrome, also known as Digital Eye Strain, refers to the vision and eye problems people can develop from prolonged computer or cell phone use; these problems include symptoms like: eyestrain, headaches, blurred vision, dry eyes, and neck/shoulder pain. While many of these symptoms are only temporary, they can be relived by following the 20/20/20 rule. This rule asks individuals working in front of a screen to take a 20-second break to view something 20 feet away every 20 minutes. For more information regarding the 20/20/20 rule, visit: https://www.aoa.org/patients-and-public/caring-for-your-vision/protecting-your-vision/computer-vision-syndrome.
